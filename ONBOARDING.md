# Welcome to the map room — a tester's guide to Lantero

Thank you for trying Lantero. You're one of the first people outside my family to use it, and your honest reaction is the whole point of this round. This guide gets you running in a few minutes and tells you what kind of feedback helps most.

You don't need to be technical, and you don't need to "finish" anything. Poke around, try a real mission with a real kid if you can, and tell me what you think.

---

## What Lantero is (in one breath)

A planner for *you*, the parent, that turns your child's interests into short, playful learning missions you run together. The child gets an adventure; you get a plan. Nothing about your child is tracked, scored, or sent anywhere — all your data stays in your own browser.

It is **not** a kids' app. Your child never logs in. The app lives with you.

## 1. Open it

Pick whichever is easiest:

- **From the link I sent you** (GitHub Pages) — just open it in a browser. This is the simplest path.
- **From the file** — open `Lantero-App/docs/index.html` by double-clicking it.

**Put it on your phone's home screen (optional but nice).** Open the GitHub Pages link in your phone browser, then "Add to Home Screen." It opens like a regular app.

A few things to know up front:

- Your data is saved **in that one browser, on that one device.** If you switch browsers or clear your history, it won't follow you — use **Settings → Data → Export** to save a backup (more on that below).
- There's no account, no sign-up, no internet required after it loads.

## 2. First run — "Welcome to the map room"

The first time you open Lantero it walks you through a short setup wizard. It's written to be done *with your child* where it makes sense, but you can also do it solo to explore. You can re-run any step later from **Settings**, so don't overthink it.

You'll be asked, roughly in order:

1. **What season is this?** — Are you using Lantero for summer/breaks, or as part of homeschooling? This sets the tone.
2. **Who is the explorer?** — Your child's name (or hero name) and age.
3. **What do they love?** — Their interests: Lego, dinosaurs, pirates, space, drawing, whatever lights them up. These become the wrappers around every mission.
4. **How do they like to learn?** — Hands-on, story-led, short bursts, etc.
5. **Where are they right now?** — A rough sense of current levels in a few skills. No tests — just your read.
6. **Life skills — what matters most right now?** — Things like confidence, public speaking, or financial literacy are treated as real subjects here.
7. **Balance and rhythm** — How much you want across different subjects.

Tip: answer honestly and quickly. Lantero keeps adjusting from your feedback as you go, so your first answers are a starting point, not a commitment.

## 3. Build and run one mission (the important part)

This is the heart of it. From the main view, find **Build a mission** and:

1. Choose a **skill** to target (or let it pick what's due).
2. Choose an **interest** wrapper.
3. Set **time** (10 / 20 / 30 min) and your child's **energy** level.
4. Choose **screen or no-screen**.

You'll get a **mission card**: a story hook, a clear goal, the materials you'll need, and a short script for you (an opening line, a couple of prompts, a praise line, and a graceful way to bail if it flops). You can **print** it.

**If you have a kid handy, actually run it.** That's where the real feedback lives. If you don't, just read the card and imagine running it — that's useful too.

When you're done, the app asks for quick feedback from both of you (did the child love it / was it too hard or easy / any notes). That's what tunes future missions.

## 4. Worth a look while you're in there

- **The ledger** (Today / This week / Month / Quarter) — where you log what you actually did.
- **The Dashboard** — a circular heat map showing "where the light is falling": which subjects are getting attention and which are being neglected.
- **The weekly pulse / north-star check** — the one question Lantero cares about most: *is your child approaching learning willingly and feeling capable?*
- **Copy AI prompt** — packages your child's profile so you can generate richer missions in Claude or ChatGPT (nothing leaves your machine unless you paste it somewhere).
- **Settings → Re-tune** — re-run any onboarding step as your child grows.

## 5. Back up your data (30 seconds)

Because everything lives in your browser, please make a backup so you don't lose your setup:

**Settings → Data → Export** saves a JSON file. To move to another device or restore later, use **Import**. If you send me feedback, attaching this file helps me see exactly what you set up.

## What I'd love feedback on

You don't have to answer all of these — even one honest reaction is gold. The most useful feedback is specific and concrete ("the mission for a 5-year-old felt too long" beats "it was fine").

**First impressions**
- In the first two minutes, did you understand what this is and who it's for?
- Did anything confuse you, or make you hesitate?

**Onboarding**
- Was the setup wizard the right length? Too long, too short?
- Did any question feel unclear or like it was asking the wrong thing?

**The mission**
- Did the mission card feel like something you'd actually run with your child?
- Was the difficulty right? Was the story hook fun, or did it feel forced?
- If you ran it for real: what happened? Did your child engage? Where did it work or break down?

**The feel**
- Does Lantero feel like a calm helper, or like one more thing to manage?
- Does it feel trustworthy with your child (no surveillance, no pressure)?

**Add / change / remove**
- What's missing that you expected?
- What felt unnecessary or got in your way?
- Would you actually use this over a summer? Why or why not?

**Anything else** — confusing words, broken buttons, ugly screens, half-formed reactions. All welcome.

## How to send it

Use the **Send feedback** button in the app header (it opens an email). If you can, attach your exported backup JSON (**Settings → Data → Export**) so I can see your setup.
